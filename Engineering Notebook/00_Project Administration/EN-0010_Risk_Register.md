# 1. Purpose

This document establishes the process for recording significant engineering decisions made throughout the ASTRA Autonomous Flight System (AAFS) project.

The Engineering Decision Log (EDL) provides a permanent record of technical decisions, their supporting rationale, alternatives considered, and resulting actions. Maintaining this record ensures traceability, supports future development, and preserves engineering knowledge throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# 2. Scope

This document applies to all major engineering decisions affecting the ASTRA program, including but not limited to:

- System architecture
- Mechanical design
- Electrical design
- Software architecture
- Flight control
- Navigation
- Communications
- Ground systems
- Simulation
- Testing
- Mission planning

Routine implementation details do not require formal decision records unless they significantly affect system performance, safety, cost, or maintainability.

---

# 3. Engineering Decision Philosophy

Engineering decisions shall be based upon objective analysis whenever practical.

Every significant decision should:

- Identify the engineering problem.
- Consider multiple viable solutions.
- Evaluate advantages and disadvantages.
- Document assumptions.
- Record supporting evidence.
- Provide clear justification.

Engineering decisions shall prioritize:

- Safety
- Reliability
- Simplicity
- Maintainability
- Performance
- Scalability

---

# 4. Decision Identification

Each engineering decision shall receive a unique identifier.

Format:

EDL-001

EDL-002

EDL-003

Decision identifiers shall never be reused.

---

# 5. Decision Record Format

Each Engineering Decision Record shall contain:

Title

Date

Author

Status

Problem Statement

Background

Alternatives Considered

Evaluation

Decision

Justification

Impacts

Affected Documents

Affected Requirements

Follow-up Actions

---

# 6. Decision Status

Engineering decisions shall use one of the following statuses:

Proposed

Under Review

Accepted

Implemented

Superseded

Rejected

Archived

---

# 7. Decision Review

Engineering decisions should be reviewed whenever:

- Requirements change.
- New information becomes available.
- Testing invalidates assumptions.
- Significant design revisions occur.

Superseded decisions shall remain archived for historical reference.
