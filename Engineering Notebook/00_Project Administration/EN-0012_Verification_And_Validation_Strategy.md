# 1. Purpose

This document defines the Verification and Validation (V&V) strategy for the ASTRA Autonomous Flight System (AAFS).

Verification and Validation activities ensure that every engineering requirement is satisfied, every subsystem performs as intended, and the complete system fulfills the objectives established by the mission.

---

# 2. Scope

This strategy applies to all ASTRA engineering activities, including:

- Systems Engineering
- Mechanical Engineering
- Electrical Engineering
- Flight Software
- Ground Control System
- Simulation
- Hardware Integration
- Flight Testing
- Mission Operations

---

# 3. Verification Philosophy

Verification confirms that engineering work satisfies documented requirements.

Every requirement shall have one or more associated verification methods.

Verification shall occur throughout development rather than only after implementation.

---

# 4. Validation Philosophy

Validation confirms that the completed system fulfills its intended operational purpose.

Validation activities shall demonstrate that ASTRA performs successfully under representative mission conditions.

---

# 5. Verification Methods

The following verification methods shall be used throughout the project.

I — Inspection

Visual examination of hardware, documentation, software, or manufactured components.

A — Analysis

Mathematical analysis, simulation, modeling, or engineering calculations.

T — Test

Controlled execution of hardware or software to demonstrate compliance.

D — Demonstration

Operational demonstration under representative mission conditions.

Each requirement shall specify at least one verification method.

---

# 6. Verification Levels

Verification activities shall be conducted at multiple levels.

Component Level

Verification of individual hardware or software components.

Subsystem Level

Verification of integrated subsystem functionality.

System Level

Verification of complete vehicle performance.

Mission Level

Verification of the integrated flight system under operational conditions.

---

# 7. Validation Activities

Validation may include:

- Flight simulation
- Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL)
- Software-in-the-Loop (SIL)
- Bench testing
- Static testing
- Ground testing
- Flight testing
- Mission evaluation

Successful validation shall demonstrate that mission objectives are satisfied.

---

# 8. Acceptance Criteria

A requirement shall be considered verified only when:

- The specified verification activity is successfully completed.
- Results satisfy the defined acceptance criteria.
- Supporting documentation is complete.
- Verification evidence has been recorded.

A subsystem shall be considered validated only after demonstrating successful operation within its intended environment.

---

# 9. Verification Matrix

A Verification Matrix shall be maintained linking:

- Requirements
- Verification Method
- Test Procedure
- Test Report
- Verification Status

The matrix shall remain current throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# 10. Responsibilities

The Project Maintainer is responsible for:

- Defining verification activities.
- Approving validation procedures.
- Reviewing verification evidence.
- Maintaining verification records.

Contributors are responsible for ensuring their work satisfies applicable requirements before submission.
