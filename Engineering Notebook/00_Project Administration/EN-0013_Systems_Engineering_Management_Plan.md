# 1. Purpose

This document defines the Systems Engineering Management Plan (SEMP) for the ASTRA Autonomous Flight System (AAFS).

The SEMP establishes the engineering lifecycle, development methodology, technical management practices, and systems engineering processes used throughout the ASTRA project.

---

# 2. Scope

This document applies to all engineering disciplines participating in ASTRA, including:

- Systems Engineering
- Mechanical Engineering
- Electrical Engineering
- Flight Software
- Ground Control System
- Simulation
- Testing
- Mission Operations

---

# 3. Systems Engineering Philosophy

ASTRA shall follow a model-based, requirements-driven systems engineering approach.

Engineering activities shall begin with clearly defined requirements, progress through design and implementation, and conclude with verification and validation.

All engineering decisions shall be documented and traceable.

---

# 4. Engineering Lifecycle

The ASTRA development lifecycle consists of the following phases:

1. Mission Definition

2. Requirements Development

3. System Architecture

4. Preliminary Design

5. Detailed Design

6. Implementation

7. Simulation

8. Integration

9. Verification

10. Validation

11. Flight Testing

12. Mission Review

Engineering activities may iterate between phases as required.

---

# 5. Technical Reviews

The following reviews should be conducted throughout development:

Mission Concept Review (MCR)

System Requirements Review (SRR)

Preliminary Design Review (PDR)

Critical Design Review (CDR)

Test Readiness Review (TRR)

Flight Readiness Review (FRR)

Post-Flight Review (PFR)

Each review shall document findings, actions, and approvals.

---

# 6. Technical Baselines

The ASTRA project shall maintain the following technical baselines:

Functional Baseline

Allocated Baseline

Product Baseline

Operational Baseline

Changes to established baselines shall follow the Configuration Management Plan.

---

# 7. Systems Engineering Activities

Primary systems engineering activities include:

- Requirements management
- System architecture development
- Interface definition
- Trade studies
- Risk management
- Configuration management
- Verification planning
- Validation planning
- Design reviews
- Mission analysis

---

# 8. Documentation Flow

Engineering documentation shall progress through the following sequence:

Mission Requirements

↓

System Requirements

↓

Subsystem Requirements

↓

Design Documentation

↓

Implementation

↓

Testing

↓

Mission Reports

All documents shall remain traceable throughout the engineering lifecycle.

---

# 9. Deliverables

Major project deliverables include:

- Engineering Notebook
- Source Code
- Hardware Designs
- CAD Models
- Simulation Models
- Test Reports
- Mission Reports
- Flight Data
- Technical Documentation

---

# 10. Continuous Improvement

Lessons learned from design reviews, simulations, testing, and flight operations shall be incorporated into future revisions of the ASTRA engineering process.

Engineering practices should evolve as the project matures while maintaining documentation integrity and traceability.
