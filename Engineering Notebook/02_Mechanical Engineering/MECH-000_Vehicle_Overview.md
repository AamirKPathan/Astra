# 1. Purpose

This document provides the high-level definition of the ASTRA vehicle.

It establishes the overall vehicle configuration, subsystem layout, design philosophy, project constraints, and preliminary performance objectives. This document serves as the primary mechanical reference for all subsequent design activities.

---

# 2. Vehicle Description

ASTRA is a modular experimental aerospace vehicle designed to serve as a platform for the development and evaluation of avionics, Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC), telemetry, and systems engineering methodologies.

The vehicle architecture emphasizes modularity, maintainability, and incremental development.

---

# 3. Vehicle Configuration

The vehicle is composed of the following major assemblies:

- Nose Cone
- Payload Bay
- Avionics Bay
- Airframe
- Recovery System
- Propulsion Interface
- Motor Mount
- Fin Assembly

---

# 4. Primary Objectives

The vehicle is intended to:

- Validate avionics hardware
- Evaluate flight software
- Collect and transmit telemetry
- Demonstrate autonomous guidance and stabilization
- Record flight data for post-flight analysis
- Support safe recovery operations
- Serve as a modular research platform

---

# 5. Design Philosophy

The mechanical design shall prioritize:

- Structural integrity
- Modularity
- Ease of maintenance
- Manufacturability
- Expandability
- Serviceability
- Documentation-first development

# 8. Preliminary Design Constraints

The vehicle shall:

- Use a modular mechanical architecture.
- Support removable subsystem modules.
- Allow access to avionics for maintenance.
- Support future subsystem expansion.
- Maintain complete engineering documentation.

---

# 9. Preliminary Performance Objectives

Performance objectives will be established and refined as the project matures.

Examples include:

- Reliable subsystem integration
- Stable flight characteristics
- Reliable telemetry
- Comprehensive data logging
- Repeatable testing
- Safe recovery
